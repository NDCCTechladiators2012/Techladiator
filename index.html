Olympic Horizon

Introduction

The 2024 Summer Olympics, officially known as the Games of the XXXIII Olympiad and branded as Paris 2024, were held from July 26 to August 11, 2024, in France, with Paris as the main host city.​ An array of events took place in 16 additional cities, including Marseille for sailing and Tahiti for surfing. Paris became the second city to host the Summer Olympics three times, marking the centenary of the Paris 1924 Games. This edition introduced breaking as an Olympic sport and achieved full gender parity among athletes. The United States led the medal count with 40 golds, closely followed by China. Notably, several nations won their first Olympic medals, including Dominica, Saint Lucia, Cape Verde, and Albania. The Games were marked by significant security measures and community involvement, as athletes were paraded by boat during the unique opening ceremony along the Seine.

History of Olympics

Summer Olympic Games, also known as the Games of the Olympics, is a prestigious international multi-sport event held every four years, organized by the International Olympic Committee (IOC).​ The inaugural Games occurred in 1896 in Athens, Greece, with a modest participation of 250 male athletes from 14 nations. Over the years, the Games have significantly expanded, hosting 339 events in 2021 with over 11,420 competitors, nearly half of whom were women, from 206 countries. Gold, silver, and bronze medals have been awarded since 1904, marking the tradition of recognizing athletic achievement. The Games have been hosted in various countries, notably the United States, Great Britain, and France, with Paris being the site in 2024. Only five nations—Australia, France, Great Britain, Greece, and Switzerland—have participated in every edition. The United States leads the all-time medal count and has topped the medal table on 19 occasions. The success of the Summer Olympics also led to the establishment of the Winter Olympic Games, further solidifying the Olympics' position as the largest and most prestigious multi-sport event globally.

Paris Olympics 2024

The Paris 2024 Summer Olympics, officially referred to as the Games of the XXXIII Olympiad, is poised to be a landmark international sporting event scheduled from July 26 to August 11, 2024. This will mark the third time Paris has hosted the Summer Olympics, previously welcoming athletes in 1900 and 1924.

 Venue and Locations:

The Games will take advantage of iconic Parisian landmarks as venues. The opening ceremony is set to take place on the River Seine, a unique feature that will allow up to 600,000 spectators to witness the event along its banks, making it the first Olympics to host the opening ceremony outside a traditional stadium.

Sustainable Practices:

One of the core objectives of the Paris 2024 Games is to prioritize sustainability. The organizing committee plans to utilize existing venues and facilities to minimize the environmental impact. Many competitions will be held in venues that are already established, ensuring that the Games leave a minimal carbon footprint.

Sports and Events:

Paris 2024 will feature a wide array of sports, including traditional events such as athletics, swimming, gymnastics, and basketball. New sports, such as breaking (breakdancing) and sport climbing, will make their Olympic debut, aligning the Games with contemporary athletic trends and interests.

Inclusivity and Legacy:

The organizers aim to create a sense of inclusivity, ensuring that the event celebrates diversity. The Games are also designed to leave a lasting legacy for sport in France, fostering increased participation in athletics and various sports at the grassroots level.

Cultural Aspect

In conjunction with the sporting events, Paris 2024 will showcase French culture through arts and cultural initiatives. Events featuring art, music, and performance will occur alongside the Olympic competitions, providing a holistic experience for visitors and participants.

Participation

Anticipating over 10,500 athletes from approximately 206 nations, the Games will have a strong emphasis on gender equality, with nearly half of the competitors expected to be women. This focus on inclusivity reflects the broader movement in sports to ensure equal representation.

Symbols of the Games

The enduring symbols of the Olympic Games, such as the Olympic Rings—representing unity and friendship among nations—will be prominent throughout the event. The Olympic Flame will also play a significant role in the festivities, symbolizing hope and inspiration.

Achivements in 2024 Olympics

The 2024 Paris Olympics, a spectacle of athletic prowess and international camaraderie, saw a surge in global sporting spirit. While the Games were a celebration of athletic achievements across diverse disciplines, the race for the coveted medals was intense, with nations vying for top spots on the medal table. The United States, a consistent powerhouse in Olympic history, emerged as the champion, claiming a staggering 40 gold medals, solidifying its position as a global sporting leader. China, known for its consistent performance in various sporting categories, secured a close second place, showcasing its dedication to athletic excellence.

Great Britain, a nation with a rich Olympic legacy, secured a commendable third place, demonstrating its prowess in a wide range of sports. France, the host nation, reveled in the spirit of the Games, securing a respectable fourth place, with its athletes achieving impressive victories on home turf. Australia, renowned for its sporting prowess, particularly in swimming and athletics, solidified its position as a top contender, claiming fifth place on the medal table.

India, a nation with a burgeoning sporting landscape, made its mark in the 2024 Paris Olympics, achieving its most successful performance at the Summer Games. While the exact ranking of the top 10 may vary slightly based on the final medal count, India’s presence in the top 10 signifies a significant milestone in its sporting journey. India’s success was fueled by its athletes’ remarkable performances in diverse disciplines, including wrestling, archery, and badminton, showcasing the country's growing athletic talent pool.

Other nations that solidified their positions in the top 10 included Germany, Japan, Italy, and Canada. These nations consistently demonstrated their athletic strength, achieving impressive medal counts across a variety of sporting events. The 2024 Paris Olympics served as a platform for these nations to showcase their athletic prowess and to inspire future generations of athletes.

The Games not only highlighted the achievements of individual athletes but also underscored the power of international collaboration and sporting spirit. The 2024 Paris Olympics, with its diverse range of athletes, disciplines, and nations, will be remembered as a celebration of human potential and athletic excellence, leaving a lasting legacy for the future of sports.

    India's Achivement in Olympics

    At the Paris 2024 Olympics, India achieved notable success, securing a total of six medals, comprising one silver and five bronze.​ This effort equaled their best haul alongside the London 2012 Olympics but fell short of expectations given their previous total of seven medals at the Tokyo 2020 Olympics. Indian athletes participated vigorously across a variety of sports and made significant strides toward success.

    1. Overall Performance

    India concluded its campaign with six medals, including five bronze and one silver, ranking 71st on the medal tally. This total is one medal less than the performance in the Tokyo 2020 Olympics, which recorded seven medals. Notably, while no gold medals were secured, the participation in numerous events showcased a strong competitive spirit among athletes.

    2. Medal Wins

    The Indian medals at the Paris 2024 Olympics came from various competitions, with Manu Bhaker being a standout athlete. She won the first medal for India, a bronze in the women's 10m air pistol event. She also teamed up with Sarabjot Singh to win another bronze in the mixed team event, marking a historic moment as she became the first Indian to secure two medals in a single Olympic campaign.

    3. Noteworthy Athlete Achievements

    Apart from Bhaker's remarkable performance, Swapnil Kusale also achieved a bronze in the men's 50m rifle 3 positions, marking India's first medal in this event. Neeraj Chopra, despite a valiant effort, secured silver in the men's javelin throw, making him the second male athlete from India to win two Olympic medals in individual events after independence. In wrestling, 21-year-old Aman Sehrawat struck bronze in the men's 57kg freestyle event, becoming India's youngest Olympic medallist .

    4. Team Events and Near Misses

    India's hockey team defended their bronze medal, showcasing their continued excellence in a sport with a rich Olympic history for the nation. However, the campaign was marred by several narrow misses, with multiple athletes finishing in fourth place, including Lakshya Sen in badminton and Saikhom Mirabai Chanu in weightlifting, highlighting the competitive nature of the events.

    5. Future Outlook

    The overall performance at the Paris 2024 Olympics indicates a positive trend in Indian sports, with many athletes close to podium finishes. Optimism surrounds future competitions, backed by impressive preparations and growing support for athletes across disciplines. The experiences gained from this Olympics are likely to bolster India's hopes for success in the upcoming Los Angeles 2028 Olympics.
